My Story

Beginning when I was young, I have always been interested in design. I can remember sitting for hours on my bedroom floor, blatantly ignoring the Lego set instructions and mashing together multiple sets into my own spaceship creations. I would build iterate and build for hours on end. Not much has changed!

Since those carefree days of youth, Iâ€™ve gotten my undergrad degree at the Rochester Institute of Technology in industrial design (physical product design) and spent the next four years working at a consultancy firm that designed and manufactured store interiors, packaging and retail fixtures for the luxury watch and jewelry industries.

On to the next adventure, I packed my bags and moved from New Jersey out to Colorado where I freelanced for five years doing industrial design work for both my previous employer as well as a number of other clients I picked up and worked with along the way.

Which brings us to today. When the pandemic hit and the retail shopping business took a major hit, I took the opportunity to learn and move into UI/UX away from industrial design. One of the things design teaches you is how to adapt to unforeseen difficulties and turn setbacks into opportunity. The slowdown in the economy from the pandemic gave me that opportunity I was looking for to learn UI/UX over a year period and make a career switch.

Freelancing as a solopreneur for those few years showed me that what I really want at this point in my career is the chance to tightly collaborate with other talented designers and stakeholders and produce great user centric work in a dynamic community of other people. The highly collaborative nature of UI/UX and the tech world world offers that and its something I want to be a part of.
