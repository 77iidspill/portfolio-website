Savr: A Responsive Recipe and Meal Planning Site

My Process

Objectives / Project Goals

The high level user goal guiding this project was to create a responsive web experience for busy mothers, as well as single professionals who like to cook and want to reduce the time and effort that goes into weekly dinner prep. At the same time they also want to pursue creativity, exploration and the aspects of cooking that they find enjoyable.

Insights from Research

I conducted a series of interviews with 4 participants. Using Google Recorder was helpful here, as I was able to fully engage while conducting the interview and pour over notes later, as I had both an audio log and written transcript with which to referernce later on when analyzing research. I also conducted a competative SWOT analysis of some existing recipe type native mobile apps and web services. The main benefit I got from this, was I was able to quickly familiarize myself with the recipe app space overall and get a sense of common UI elements used, problems that showed up in multiple instances and understand the dominant aesthetic themes common to the space.

Developing Personas

MVP Creation

Having been convinced via my interview sessions and secondary research that my original overall direction was generally confirmed, I was confident that I was on the right track. My research did however point out some specific things that would be important in determining which tasks to focus on. I learned that weekly meal prep is often ad hoc and is not set in stone, as plans change throughout the week. I learned that there is often apprehension on the part of mothers especially, to cook something new when unsure if the rest of the family will enjoy it. I learned that the enjoyable aspects of food shopping are generally in browsing fresh produce, meats and cheeses because of its serendipitous nature.

Prototype Additional Testing and Results

In sketching a new set of flows, I focused specifically on the calendar and shopping lists flows as most of the issues uncovered via the tests were located there. While keeping the rest of the flows largely unchanged, there were a few slight changes I made that would better resize in a scalable web application on tablet and desktop screens.

Initial Wireframes and Breakpoints

With the XS mobile screen layout starting to take shape, I also began to develop the larger S, M, and L breakpoints for the home screen and calendar screen so they could evolve along with the XS mobile versions at this early stage.

UI Development

Development of the overall tone of the app initially started along two distinct aesthetic directions as seen in the below moodboards. The first idea was to use a blue monochrome theme because of blue’s perceptively calming, reassuring charactertics. The second idea was to use warm colors and natural textures to evoke more of a “home cooking” aesthetic.

Pulling Together the MVP

The last step was to pull it all together into a package that could stand on its own out in the real world. At this point I was confident that the core flows that made up the experience would serve the personas needs and goals well and would be a good starting off point for developing the product in the future.
